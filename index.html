My Top Five Pet Peeves
& Their Justifications

Since you asked!

Littering

More than just laziness, littering is a monumental social injustice too grave to be defined as merely a pet peeve. Costly time and manpower are necessary to abate the unsightly problem. What's hard about picking up after yourself?

Walking Two (or More) Abreast on a Sidewalk

Humans love to travel in packs, and frankly, sidewalks weren't designed for us. Even on a generously wide, ADA-compliant sidewalk, more than two adults cannot walk side-by-side. It's simple: we have to move into single-file formation on the sidewalk to avoid the dreaded game of "sidewalk chicken."

Back Seat Driving

As someone who reflexively follows commands, this one is a nightmare. I will obey a backseat driver almost without thought, until the angry moment I realize I'm not in control of my own vehicle. Dangerous habit.

Overfamiliar Behavior

While touch is the most grievous offense in this category, I would go so far as to proclaim that simple overuse of my first name is downright fresh. I like a nice, stiff, formal address, and hands where I can see them.

Indecision

For some reason, when ordering in restaurants particularly, this one mortifies me. Your server's time is just as valuable to them as yours is to you. Don't know what you want? Simply ask your server for more time. But please don't force them to stand by while you grapple with your fear of better options.
